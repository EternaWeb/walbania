We process personal information for the following purposes:

- coordinating hotels, guides, transport, activities and other suppliers;
- processing and recording payments, refunds and cancellations;

## 5. Legal bases for processing

Depending on the circumstances, we rely on one or more of the following legal bases:

- **Contract:** processing is necessary to prepare, enter into or perform your travel booking or another agreement with you.
- **Legal obligation:** processing is necessary to comply with tax, accounting, consumer-protection, security or other applicable law.
- **Legitimate interests:** processing is necessary for reasonable business purposes such as responding to enquiries, securing our website, preventing fraud, managing suppliers, improving services and defending legal claims, where those interests are not overridden by your rights.
- **Consent:** you have clearly agreed to a specific use, such as optional marketing cookies or email marketing, and may withdraw that consent.
- **Vital interests:** processing is reasonably necessary to protect someone’s life or physical safety in an emergency.

## 6. Health, accessibility and other sensitive information

Information about health, allergies, accessibility or dietary requirements may reveal sensitive personal information. We process it only when necessary to safely arrange or provide your requested service and when a lawful condition permits, including explicit consent where required, protection of vital interests, or establishment and defence of legal claims.

We limit this information to what is reasonably necessary and share it only with people who need it to arrange or safely provide the service. You may withdraw consent where consent is the legal basis, but this may prevent us or a supplier from safely providing part of the booking.

## 10. How long we keep information

We retain personal information only for as long as reasonably necessary for the purposes described in this policy, including legal, accounting, tax, safety and dispute-resolution requirements.

Typical retention periods are:

| Information | Typical retention |
| --- | --- |
| Unsuccessful enquiries and quotations | Up to 24 months after the last meaningful contact |
| Booking, invoice and payment records | For the period required by applicable accounting and tax law |
| Operational traveller information | For the trip and a reasonable period afterward, normally up to 24 months |
| Sensitive health or accessibility information | Only as long as necessary for the trip, incident handling or a legal claim |

These periods may be extended where required by law, necessary for a dispute or requested by a competent authority. Information may be deleted or anonymised earlier when it is no longer needed.

## 16. Cookies and similar technologies

Cookies are small files stored on your device. Similar technologies may include local storage, pixels and software development tools.

Wonder Albania aims to use a limited number of cookies. Our current categories are:

### Strictly necessary cookies

These support essential functions such as:

- website security;
- load balancing and reliable page delivery;
- remembering privacy or cookie choices;
- maintaining a booking or account session; and
- preventing fraud.

These technologies are used because the website cannot operate properly without them. They are not used for behavioural advertising.

### Preference cookies

If enabled, these remember choices such as language, currency, location or display preferences. Where required, they will be used only after consent.

### Analytics cookies

If we enable analytics, they may help us understand aggregated website usage and diagnose performance problems. Where consent is legally required, analytics will remain disabled until you accept them.

### Marketing cookies

We do not currently intend to use behavioural advertising cookies. If we later introduce advertising pixels or cross-site tracking, we will update this policy and obtain consent where required before activating them.

Withdrawing cookie consent does not make earlier consent-based processing unlawful. Any preference tool should make rejecting optional cookies as easy as accepting them.
